Scorecard

Amazon

F

Direct Emissions: 5% higher emissions compared to other retailers used in operations and shipping.

Indirect Emissions: 40% more exposure to commodities that drive deforestation (palm oil, soy, beef, timber) compared to the average retailer. Deforestation causes about 15% of global emissions, and is a major source of indirect emissions within retailers’ supply chains.

Policies: (1) Committed to sourcing 100% RSPO-certified palm oil for its in-house brand, but it has not reported any progress against this commitment or attempted to increase certification outside of its private label. (2) Sells about 150 million lbs. of beef annually but has no sustainability commitment around beef. (3) Has declined to respond to CDP’s Forest survey on forest risk commodities, despite being requested to do so by investors.

Popular Products: Membership

Alternatives: Sam’s Club membership (owned by Walmart), Amazon Prime membership
